Mississauga's biggest hackathon is here.

FraserHacks is Mississauga's largest high school hackathon organized entirely by students at John Fraser Secondary School. More than 100 students from all of Peel Region will be coming together on February 28, 2019, for a full 10 hours of friendly hacking, enthusiastic learning, and delicious food.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to know how to code to participate?

FraserHacks welcomes people of all coding backgrounds—even if that means none at all! We will have a variety of workshops and mentors at the event to help you write your first line of code.

What’s the cost to attend?

Absolutely nothing! There is no cost to attend FraserHacks thanks to the generous contributions from our sponsors.

Is this a teamed event?

Yes! FraserHacks will be allowing teams of up to 4 people. But don’t sweat if you don’t have a team coming into the event—there will be a chance to meet other “single” hackers at the beginning of the event.

Who is eligible to participate?

Students from all of Peel Region are encouraged to apply to attend FraserHacks. All students are welcome!

What should I bring to the event?

Laptops, chargers, and a student card or other form of ID are required for the event. But feel free to bring whatever else makes you comfortable!

What if I have another question?

Feel free to email us at contact@fraserhacks.ca, or send a message to our social media pages.

What if I’m not a high school student?

You can still participate! We’d love to have you as a mentor! You can register here.

What kind of workshops and activities will be hosted?

We’re hosting a variety of workshops from coding basics to working with artificial intelligence. There will always be something exciting to learn! In addition, we have a ton of fun activities planned for you such as e-sports tournaments, nugget-eating contests, and more! FraserHacks is much more than just a hackathon – we make sure that it’s an event you’ll thoroughly enjoy.
